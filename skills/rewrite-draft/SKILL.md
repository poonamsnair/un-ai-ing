# rewrite-draft

Use this when you receive an un-AI-ing "rewrite request" packet. Your job here is to plan and produce **draft v1** — not the final answer.

## Steps

1. Read the whole packet: the source document context, draft text with `[FIX]`/`[DELETE]` markup, reviewer comments, style references, hard constraints, and writing principles.
2. Write a short brief as **private working notes** (do not output it):
   - Audience, and what they already know.
   - Purpose and the central claim to preserve.
   - Non-negotiable facts, numbers, terms, and uncertainty to keep exactly.
   - The source HTML/CSS scaffold to preserve, if provided.
   - The structure the piece should follow.
   - Every reviewer mark you must satisfy (comments, highlights, style references, scribbled deletions).
3. Fix structure first. If the order of ideas is weak, reorder before line-editing.
4. Write **draft v1**: apply each reviewer mark in context and write to the standard in `skills/writing-principles` (the 100 keys and non-negotiables). Keep every fact.
5. Hand draft v1 to `rewrite-verify`. Do not save or return yet.

## Rules

- Do not invent claims, evidence, thresholds, citations, or examples.
- Accuracy beats elegance. Keep a passive construction, long sentence, or technical term when it improves precision or focus.
- Treat this as a draft. Expect at least two revision rounds before anything is final.
